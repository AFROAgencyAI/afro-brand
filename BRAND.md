# AFRO Agency AI — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Name

AFRO is the brand name. It is not a client-facing acronym.

"Acquisition, Fulfilment, Retention, Operations" is an internal architecture reminder. It describes the four service domains Tyler covers. It should never appear in client-facing hero copy and never be explained unprompted to prospects.

Where the acronym meaning can appear: internal documentation, the About page (if someone goes looking), footer as a subtle brand descriptor, social bios.

If someone asks "what does AFRO stand for?", the answer is a brand story: "It stands for the four things most agencies ignore. They focus on acquisition and forget everything after the click. We cover the full cycle: how you acquire, how you deliver, how you retain, and how it all runs."

---

## Brand Truth

AFRO Agency AI exists because most med spas deliver exceptional care but communicate their value poorly, and most agencies only fix the acquisition pipe without touching the real problem. AFRO builds the full revenue system: the words, the design, the follow-up, the proof of what worked. Every decision is governed by what the data actually says, not what is convenient to report.

**Core belief:** Marketing is the entire revenue cycle, not just lead generation. Agencies that only touch acquisition are selling an incomplete product.

**Non-negotiable value:** Truth-seeking. Show clients exactly which ads produced paying patients. Refuse to run campaigns the data says will not work. Tell a client their positioning is wrong even when it is uncomfortable.

---

## Positioning

AFRO is the only med spa marketing partner that builds your system before asking for a commitment, then proves what worked every month with real revenue data. No contracts. No guessing. No trust required upfront because the work speaks first.

**Primary competitor:** DIY/inaction. Med spa owners cobbling together Canva, a GHL template, and their front-desk person. AFRO competes against "I'll just keep doing it myself," not against other agencies.

**Category conventions AFRO breaks:**

1. Build first, pitch later. The website is pre-built before the prospect commits.
2. 7-day free trial, cancel anytime. No long contracts.
3. Monthly attribution reports with real numbers. No cherry-picked case studies.

**Emotional territory:** Relief. After months of guessing, the client finally sees exactly what is working.

## Audience

### Persona 1: The Burned Scaler

Owner-operator with 1-2 locations, 2-5 years in, actively looking to grow but gun-shy from a bad agency experience. Wants proof, not promises. The 7-day free trial addresses this directly. Real conversion happens when the first monthly report shows which specific ads produced paying patients.

### Persona 2: The Uncertain Expander

Owner-operator doing $1-2M/year, ready to open location 2 or 3, unsure if marketing will scale with them. Needs a repeatable system, not a freelancer. Evaluating whether AFRO is a vendor or a growth partner.

### Anti-Persona (actively repel)

- New operators (under 1 year): no baseline to optimize
- Tech-resistant operators requiring high-touch human interaction
- Operators with a full in-house marketing team
- Corporate/multi-location chains (10+ locations)

## Messaging

### Core Message

"We build your marketing system before you pay a dollar, then show you exactly which ads produce paying patients every month."

### Proof Points

1. **Proof-first model.** Website built before you commit. No contract, no fee, no obligation.
2. **Attribution clarity.** Every click tracked from ad to appointment. No vanity metrics.
3. **Speed-to-lead.** SMS in seconds. Email in minutes. Missed call text-back from day one.
4. **Click-to-Book Optimization.** Headline, copy, UI, chat widget engineered to convert visitors into booked appointments.
5. **One system, not a vendor stack.** Website, lead capture, follow-up, attribution, reporting integrated.

### Tone

- **Direct.** Say what is true. No hedging, no fluff.
- **Calm confidence.** Not loud, not salesy. The proof-first model means AFRO shows, not convinces.
- **Warm precision.** Human and approachable. Every claim has a number behind it.
- **Respectful of the owner's intelligence.** These are $1M+ business owners. Do not condescend.

### Language: Use

- "Here's what the data shows"
- "Booked patients" (not "leads generated")
- "Your system" (ownership language)
- "We built this for you"
- Specific numbers: response times, percentages, dollar amounts
- "Click-to-Book Optimization"

### Language: Never Use

- "Cutting-edge AI"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Full-service agency"
- "Revolutionize" / "disrupt" / "game-changing"
- "Leverage" / "utilize" (say "use")
- "ROI-driven"
- "Growth partner" (before they are a client)
- "Bespoke" / "tailored solutions"
- "Seamless"
- "Scale your business" without specifics
- "We're different"
- Em dash constructions

## Visual Identity

### Design Philosophy

**Premium but accessible.** AFRO looks like a serious, high-caliber partner while remaining warm and approachable. **Current, not trendy.** Clean, well-crafted design that will not look dated in 2 years. No startup theater. No 2019 WordPress templates.

### Color Palette

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Orange | `#FF8000` | Energy, action, CTAs, brand mark. The signature. |
| Dark Orange | `#CC6600` | Hover/active states. Logo extrusion depth. |
| Cream | `#FAF9F5` | Warm neutral background. Sections off from white. |
| Black | `#141413` | Headlines, primary text. Warm black, never pure `#000`. |
| Body Gray | `#5A5850` | Body copy. Warm, readable. |
| Secondary Gray | `#8A877D` | Captions, metadata, supporting text. |
| Border | `#DDD9D0` | Card edges, dividers. Subtle, warm. |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary background. |

### Gradients

Radial gradients are allowed on section/container backgrounds with rounded edges for Apple-like bezel depth. Buttons are flat color only. No gradients on text.

### Typography

**Gilroy** (self-hosted in this repo). Geometric sans-serif. Friendly but structured.

Use weight contrast (not size alone) for hierarchy: Bold for headlines, Medium for subheads, Regular for body.

### Spacing Scale

Base unit: 8px. Geometric progression.

`4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96`

Common mappings: button padding (12/16), card padding (24/32), section gap (64/96). Always pick from the scale, never freehand.

### Radius Scale

Three tiers.

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `sm` | `4px` | Inputs, tags, small elements |
| `md` | `8px` | Cards, buttons |
| `lg` | `16px` | Modals, hero images, featured containers |

Do not exceed 16px unless using full-round (pill shape) for badges or toggles.

### Logo

The 4-diamond logo mark is sacred. Exact geometry, spacing, and color are never modified. See `README.md` in this repo for file variants, sizes, and usage rules.

### Photography and Illustration

No stock photos of smiling med spa clients. Instead: abstract patterns, clean UI screenshots of dashboards/reports, the diamond logo mark as a graphic element. If human imagery is needed, candid/editorial style only.

### Visual Don'ts

- Pure black (`#000000`) anywhere
- Cool-toned grays or blues
- Glassmorphism, heavy drop shadows, "tech startup" trends
- Stock photography of generic med spa interiors/clients
- Decorative or script fonts
- Neon or saturated colors beyond the orange family
- Busy backgrounds or textures competing with content
